# Biggest Challenges So Far

### 1. Absorbing new technical concepts while implementing them live
**Status:** Ongoing, but I have a working strategy.
Following a session and hands-on typing/building at the same time works most of the time, but it breaks down specifically when a genuinely new concept and live execution land simultaneously, like on the MongoDB provisioning day, where trying to understand something brand new and type it correctly at the same pace as the session became overwhelming. What worked: instead of forcing understanding mid-session on those specific occasions, I screenshot everything as it happens and rebuild the whole thing myself afterward, slowly, line by line. This has consistently worked better than trying to follow and understand at the same time in those moments, I've fully understood and fixed real bugs solo that I couldn't process live.

### 2. Performance under formal evaluation
**Status:** Ongoing, real improvement made.
Formal assessment conditions create pressure that doesn't reflect my actual working ability, this isn't about competence, since I execute reliably under pressure in live troubleshooting and hands-on scenarios. Under a testing/evaluation format specifically, the instinct is to move fast and finish rather than deliberate, which has cost marks on questions I otherwise understood well. Reviewing past results confirmed the gap wasn't knowledge but pacing under that pressure. What's helped: a short, mechanical checklist to run through before submitting, so slowing down is a fixed procedure rather than something that requires sitting and second-guessing.

### 3. Understanding concepts faster than I can implement them
**Status:** Ongoing, real improvement made.
I tend to grasp the logic behind something well before I can reliably produce it myself from a blank file under pressure. The gap isn't knowledge, it's the jump from "I get why this works" to actually building it live. What's helped most is going line-by-line rather than trying to write or configure a whole solution at once, it's made the implementation gap noticeably smaller over the past few weeks.
